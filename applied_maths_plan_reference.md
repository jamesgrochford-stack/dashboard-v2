## The Three Non-Negotiables

1. **Pause before every worked example — attempt it yourself first.** Watching Kieran Mills solve a problem creates the feeling of understanding. Attempting it before he does reveals whether you actually can.

2. **Do every exercise in the book independently before moving to the next lesson.** All questions, in your own handwriting. The exercise is the lesson — the video is the warm-up.

3. **Do the Old LC Questions at the end of each chapter before moving to the next topic.** These are the actual exam. Mark against examinations.ie. Write one sentence on what the scheme rewarded that you missed.

## Session Structure (Every AM block)

1. Open Grinds360 video. Don't open the book yet.
2. Watch until first worked example begins. Pause. Write the question in your notebook. Attempt it yourself — book closed, video paused.
3. Finish the video. Close it. One pass is enough.
4. Do the exercise in the Grinds360 book — independently, all questions, in your own handwriting.
5. Mark your exercise against the answers. Circle anything wrong — write what the error was, not just a cross.
6. Log the lesson complete on dashboard (+1 lesson). Note circled questions in notebook: "Ch1 L3 — Q4 wrong (simultaneous equations setup)".

## Checkpoint Session Structure

1. Review notebook notes from the chapter — re-attempt any circled questions (max 10 min total, 5 min per question)
2. Open examinations.ie — do 2 Old LC Questions timed (15 min each), no notes, no book
3. Mark against the official marking scheme
4. Write one sentence: what did the scheme reward that you missed?
5. Keep one notebook page per chapter as checkpoint log

**Threshold:** If you score below 70% on both questions, do a third before moving to the next topic.

## Topic Notes

### Ch1 — Uniform Accelerated Motion
SUVAT equations transfer directly from Physics. Move through L1–5 confidently — they're consolidation. L6 (V-t graphs) and L7 (Dimensional Analysis) deserve full attention. V-t graphs: area under curve = displacement, gradient = acceleration. Sign convention: define positive direction at the start of every UAM question.

### Ch2 — Vectors
i and j notation, dot products, and component resolution are genuinely new. L3 (i and j Vectors) has two exercises — do both. This chapter feeds directly into Ch3 L4, Ch7, and Ch8 L4. If i and j notation isn't solid after the checkpoint, review L3 before starting Projectiles.

### Ch3 — Projectiles
UAM in two dimensions simultaneously. Horizontal: constant velocity. Vertical: SUVAT with g. Write both sets of equations at the start of every question before solving anything. L2 (proving general formulae) comes up as a derivation question on the LC — you must derive R = u²sin2θ/g and H = u²sin²θ/2g from first principles, not just quote them. L4 (i and j approach) is new territory even with Physics background.

### Ch4 — Newton's Laws
Draw a free-body diagram for every question — without exception. Write F = ma for each object separately, then combine. Pulleys (L3): one object up, one down — correct sign convention for each is the most common error. If Pulleys checkpoint score is below 70%, do a third question specifically on pulleys.

### Ch6 — Work & Energy
W = Fd cosθ. KE = ½mv². PE = mgh. Conservation of energy + Newton's Laws combined is an LC exam favourite. Where friction is involved, energy is lost to heat — account for it explicitly.

### Ch7 — Circular Motion
Least Physics overlap. Key equations: ω, v = rω, a = rω² = v²/r, F = mv²/r. Write these before any question. Horizontal circles (conical pendulum): tension has vertical component (balances weight) and horizontal component (centripetal). Vertical circles: the direction of the normal reaction changes depending on whether the object is on the inside or outside of the loop — draw the diagram every time and identify which case applies before writing any equation. Inside of loop at top: centripetal = weight + normal. Outside of loop (e.g. top of a hill): centripetal = weight − normal. Getting this wrong is the most common vertical circle error.

### Ch8 — Collisions
Two equations per collision: momentum conservation + Newton's Law of Restitution (e = speed of separation / speed of approach). Label directions clearly before every question. Multiple collisions: work sequentially — each collision's output is the next collision's input. Oblique collisions (L4): resolve into components first — component parallel to wall unchanged, component perpendicular obeys NLR. This is the single step the whole solution depends on.

## When Stuck

1. Write down everything you know about the question — given values, what you're finding, which formula might connect them.
2. Re-read the lesson section in the Grinds360 book (not the video). Find the worked example closest to your question. See how it's set up.
3. If still stuck after 10 minutes — mark it, move on, come back next session. Bring persistent gaps to the Applied Maths project chat.
