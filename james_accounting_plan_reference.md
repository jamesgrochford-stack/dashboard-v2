## Diagnosis

**Core problem:** Conceptual understanding gap. Not a time, checklist, or knowledge problem. James watches the teacher solve questions and follows along (borrowed confidence), then cannot reconstruct the logic independently. This is the same pattern as Chemistry but more severe — Chemistry was exam technique; Accounting is foundational conceptual understanding.

**Evidence (school teaching order: Depreciation → Revaluation → Creditor Control → Debtor Control → Suspense → Club Accounts → Service Firms → Sole Traders):**
- Depreciation ~100% — most taught topic, highest class time, procedural/mechanical
- Revaluation 59% — conceptual extension of depreciation, dropped sharply
- Suspense 84% — inflated (recognised the question from same-day study)
- Club Accounts 60% — unfamiliar account structure
- Service Firms 53% — lowest result, most complex workings, hardest topic attempted
- Summer exam 62% — lucky with topics (depreciation + service firm)

**Key insight:** Service Firms at 53% is the lowest and most challenging result. All class tests used previously-seen or class-worked questions to some degree, so results are systematically somewhat inflated relative to genuine independent ability.

**Root cause:** Teacher teaches via board demonstration of past paper questions (OL first, then HL). Students copy down method. End-of-topic exam uses the same question worked in class. James can follow a path laid by someone else; he cannot build the path himself.

**The fix:** Grinds360 physical book first (build mental model) → Grinds360 video second (reinforce) → independent cold attempt every Thursday (test transfer). Never watch the solution before attempting.

**No grinds teacher:** James has decided not to pursue one-to-one grinds. The mitigation is rigorous self-diagnosis on Thursdays — not just noting missed figures but writing the faulty reasoning explicitly for each one. Pattern repeats across weeks signal a persistent conceptual error; fix by re-reading the Grinds360 book section from scratch, not just re-attempting the question.

**Note on method consistency:** Grinds360 sometimes uses a different account layout to the school teacher. Both are accepted by the LC marking scheme. Grinds360 is the primary method for this plan — use it consistently.

## Session Method

**Two sessions per week: Tuesday (Understand) + Thursday (Attempt)**

### Tuesday — Understand (50 min)

1. Read Grinds360 physical book section for the topic (15 min). Ask: why does each figure exist in this account? Don't move forward until the logic is clear.
2. Watch relevant Grinds360 video — pause before each adjustment, attempt it, then watch the solution (20 min).
3. Build or update the topic checklist in a dedicated notebook (10 min).
4. Log session at row-fysk.vercel.app/study.html (2 min): topic, what clicked, one open question.

**Rule:** Do not attempt a past paper question on Tuesday. Tuesday is for understanding only.

### Thursday — Attempt (50 min)

1. Set up question and timer. Write topic checklist from memory before starting (2 min).
2. Attempt the full question cold — no notes, no book (30–35 min). When stuck, write exactly what you can't figure out and move on.
3. Mark against official examinations.ie scheme (10 min). Circle every missed figure. Was it on the checklist? Write the faulty reasoning: "I thought [X] because [Y], but the correct answer is [Z] because [W]."
4. Write one sentence: what did the scheme reward that you didn't give it? (3 min)
5. Log at row-fysk.vercel.app/study.html: score, what scheme caught, one-sentence learning (2 min).

**Rule:** Never look at the scheme mid-attempt. Partial marks count — complete what you can.

**Pattern watch:** If the same type of figure appears in the one-sentence learning three weeks in a row, the mental model for that concept is still wrong. Fix by re-reading the Grinds360 book section from scratch — not just re-attempting the question.
